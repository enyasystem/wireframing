# Introduction to Wireframing

## What is Wireframing?

Wireframing is the process of creating a simple visual representation or blueprint of a digital product, such as a website or mobile app, before the actual design or development begins.  
It focuses on the layout, structure, and placement of elements like buttons, menus, and content — without worrying about colors, fonts, or images.

In simple terms, a wireframe is a *skeleton* that shows how a product will work and how users will interact with it.

---

## Importance of Wireframing in the Design Process

1. **Clarifies Structure and Functionality**  
   - Wireframes help designers and stakeholders understand how the product’s layout and navigation will function.

2. **Saves Time and Resources**  
   - Identifying layout or usability issues early in wireframes prevents costly redesigns later in development.

3. **Improves Collaboration**  
   - Wireframes provide a shared visual reference that helps designers, developers, and clients stay aligned.
